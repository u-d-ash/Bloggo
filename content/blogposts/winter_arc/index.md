I think the best December of my life is behind me. Life is never again going to present me an opportunity to roam around the country to engage in my favourite activities with my crew, while being completely carefree.

Maybe, even if it does, miraculously, I won't be 22 again.

Winter of 2025 was a big bucket of memories. Here are some splashes...

### twenty second

My birthday party was a heavy dinner at Lauriat, the most premium dining experience IITG can offer, with four of my close friends. This outing was a much needed break given that it was only a week to go before Day 1 placement interviews. At that time, companies were rolling out shortlists pretty much every other hour and the unemployed would throng to that one poorly managed spreadsheet to check their fate...

{{< figure src="meme1.png" align="center" width="300">}} 

Navi's shortlist arrived while we were there, peacefully sipping our mocktails. My name was there - my friends got excited - me, not so much. It is always good to have one more interview under your belt but that company was anything but my aspiration.

A loud knock woke me up the next morning. It was Shreyansh who was just returning from an assessment.

> *"Tera Google me naam aa gya."*

I was elated. Before the placement season had even begun, I was targeting Google and this shortlist was one step closer to achieving that.

### fifth

I had three shortlists for the first interview slot (0000 to 0600) : Google, Navi & Meesho. My plan was to give Google's first round and then go for Meesho. Who cares about Navi?

The first round at Google went flawless, and it immediately followed with the second one. I didn't go for Meesho because that wasn't my first choice and I just wanted to be done with Google's process and get the potential offer as soon as possible.

My performance in the second round was okayish. *"You need to clutch it, c'mon!"* I told myself as I headed for the final round after a short 15 min break, during which I had inhaled some bananas.

I don't feel like talking about my third round. It was abysmal.

I was done with the Google process by around 0400. Meesho, AmEx and a few others had already extended offers by then and some of my hostel mates were already placed. I was sorry about my situation but seeing my friends succeed was a pleasant feeling.

The Navi representative was asking me to show up for their interview and since I had nowhere else to go, I went. The sun was already up after I was done with their first round and I was completely lethargic by then. My brain struggled to function and I just wanted to get the hell out of those corridors. I started bombing the interviews to allow them to throw me out early.

> **Interviewer** : *I see that you don't have any web development projects. You do realise that you'd be working with web stacks extensively in this role. Are you even slightly interested in this domain?*
> 
>  **Me** : *If I had found it interesting before, I would have explored it and there would've been a project or two on my resume.*

I came back to my room at around 0700, unplaced. Things had not turned out to be the way I expected them to be. My friends were expecting me to clear the Google rounds - they felt that I deserved to get in. I was able to sense the worry in their voice when their calls came in at around 0600.

I slept for an hour or so, stuffed some more bananas and was again en route to the battlefield. *"What's gone is gone, it's time to focus on the next point"*, I told myself.

The second slot began and I found myself eliminated out of two companies within a matter of hours. My brain had stopped functioning. I was not able to comprehend anything that the interviewers were trying to ask, let alone solving the problem; the sleeplessness was hitting hard.

Blackrock representative called me for the interview so I went there to try my luck. Two rounds of very open ended discussions followed. Questions as random as "How would one explain the concept of multidimensionality to a five year old kid?" were asked while I was waiting for actual problems to solve. There was a hubbub near a desk hosting two HR executives - the natural next step after I was done.

> *"Are you giving out offers immediately?"*
>
> *"What's your name?"*
>
> *"Udit Jethva"*
>
> *"Wait, Udit, it's looking positive for you ... Congratulations! You have made it to an offer!"*

{{< figure src="meme2.jpg" align="center" width="200">}} 

It was around 1230 now. My mind was exhausted to the point that I was not able to process any feelings. I pulled up a weak smile, shook some hands, received a goodie box and headed out, calling my Mom on the way. Jatin, who had made it to AmEx a few hours back, already in his casuals, was the first one to spot me with a Blackrock branded box.

A new message made its way to our hostel group chat. *"Udit Blackrock Sorted !"*.  Endorphins spiked as I scrolled through my unread messages to the news of many more of my friends getting through.

I crashed back to my room. Udbhav ordered a pizza for my lunch. I slept till evening.

It was over. I survived IIT placements.

### fifty eighth

#### trip

It was the first dinner with my tennis teammates and coach after the interviews. Sir said that it had been a while since the last time he had seen me this relieved. I went on to narrate my story and the precarious nature of this affair was successful in stressing out the sophomores, especially Idhaa. Sir, on the other hand, now expects insider trading tips after I join.

Days until our flight to Chennai were mostly about practice drills in the evenings and having good mess food, courtesy of the sports board. One Saturday was an exception though, when we went out for Zootopia 2, discovered it was Sibi's birthday, had a scrumptious meal with a diverse array of drinks, wolfed down a dozen of brownies and cheesecakes which were paid for by our fake five star reviews, went to ghat, drank some more, shared a few stories, danced like no one was watching and then finally called it a day.

Around a week later, after a three hour flight in the morning, we found ourselves outside the domestic terminal at Chennai Airport. The rough plan for the day was to roam around the city and somehow reach Tirupati by evening. Sibi, as expected[^1], took the role of our guide for the day and first took us to the underrated (hence, cleaner) Besant Nagar Beach...

{{< figure src="beach.jpg" align="center" width="300">}}

Years had passed since the last time I had been to a beach - in fact, I had forgotten what it was like to be at one. We spent hours getting washed by the waves in the windy weather, bought a few Bisleris to flush the sandy aftermath within our clothes, had a refreshing tender coconut and then went for lunch...

{{< figure src="food.jpg" align="center" width="450">}}

During this time, we had figured out to catch an evening train to reach Tirupati. When we reached the station, it was easy to spot a few other contingents who were showing up for the events at IIT Madras. We got onboard the train and bid adieu to Chennai by sunset.

Since it was a short two hour ride, we did not bother to go beyond the general sleeper ticket and that "cost" us in a different manner. We were expecting to relax a bit after a long day and maybe play a few games to pass the time, however, fate had other plans...

{{< figure src="train.jpg" align="center" width="300" title="god save indian railways">}}

The organising committee was supposed to receive us at the station but they got busy picking up some other contingent and asked us to wait an hour. It is difficult to be patient when you are in desperate need of some good dinner and a comfortable bed to crash onto. We hired an SUV and began the final 30 km stretch to the campus.

If I remember correctly, it was around 10 pm when we reached the front gates which were in the middle of nowhere. We picked up our IDs, logged our entry and entered the premises. We (boys[^2]) finally reached our allotted hostel at around 10:30 pm. A banner sporting the mascot, Tejas, was hanging near the doors...

> IIT Tirupati welcomes you to the 58th Inter IIT Sports Meet

#### tirupati

The first task after arrival was to get some food. We tried seeking some help from the volunteer who had shown us the way to the hostel from the main gates.

> *"The mess has closed now, but there are some refreshments in your room."*
> 
> *"What refreshments?"*
>
> *"One paper boat and a choco pie..."*

We were pissed. We did not even try to hide it. 

The volunteer was able to sense it and comforted us by saying that they would get us food delivered from a not-so-closeby restaurant and asked us to place an order. We head to our dual sharing rooms on the ground floor - decently spacious, clean and comfortable beds, basic toiletries and refreshments lying on the table and of course, wifi...

{{< figure src="card.jpg" align="center" width="300">}}

Extremely sound sleep followed after a cold shower and a not-so-great dinner.

It was a string of inconveniences and disappointments after we had just arrived but I don't think I can complain about anything that followed after that[^3]. Food wise, the mess blew my expectations : *"It was perfect. Perfect. Down to the last minute detail."* The campus didn't have a lot to offer except four newly-built hard tennis courts, a cozy amphitheatre near the boys' hostel and deserted rolling roads wider than Guwahati. The weather was pleasant, warm in the daytime but soothing cool in the evenings and the surrounding hills composed a scenic backdrop for the matches...

{{< figure src="court.jpg" align="center" width="300">}}

#### tournament

> "You'll be the water boy" - Sir

After years of failed trials, I had finally made it to the team in the final year, as the third guy. You don't need a third guy to play the matchups in the tournament because every matchup consists of two singles and one doubles. Sibi and Pranav were going to be our aces and to be honest, I didn't expect to get on the courts for any match. I was prepared to be the water boy.

Boys had Kanpur, Gandhinagar & Jodhpur in their group, while girls had Kanpur, Hyderabad & BHU. Kanpur was common for boys and girls.

Team IIT Kanpur was an absolute unit. Both of their teams - rock solid and the boys were defending this year. Girls tennis throughout the tournament was *haramball* [^4] except that one girl from Kanpur who was the only one playing the game, the way it was supposed to be.

**matchday 1**

Boys had their first match against Kanpur and as expected, we were defeated in straight matches. Sibi and Pranav, while great players, were inexperienced in Inter IIT and Kanpur's experience was reflected in their game. That day, girls had their match against BHU which was a cake walk.

**matchday 2**

Boys were up against Gandhinagar next. This was a virtual knockout match because it was apparent that Jodhpur was going to be "belted" [^5] by everyone and Kanpur was going to belt everyone in the group. Sibi had picked up an injury from his singles against Kanpur so Pranav was going to play the first singles.

Gandhinagar's player was a consistent one who knew how to rally well and keep balls while also going for winners at times. He was almost as good as Pranav in skill levels, to be honest. It was going to be a good match.

If I remember correctly Gandhinagar had picked the first 2 games in the first set and were leading 2-0. Pranav geared up then and after a great fight, the set concluded 6-4 in our favour. It was looking good in the second set as well until this damned game when Pranav was serving - there were at least 10 deuces. Tennis serves are physically exhausting to execute and one could easily notice that Pranav was going out of fuel as the game proceeded. He eventually got broken and was never able to recover in that set. Second set 4-6.

In the third set, the match was going neck to neck as both the players had resorted to "haramball" tactics to save their energy. It was not a delight to spectate but I enjoyed screaming and cheering at the top of my lungs. The final set's scoreline eventually evolved to 5-4 with Pranav serving.

The match had gotten attention from everyone who was on the courts that time, given how close it was going and the crowd now was more than just Gandhinagar people and us. The Kanpur boys team was nearby and they were betting on who was going to make it. I remember this game very clearly. Pranav was trailing 0-40 and it looked like the set was going to be extended. Next followed three cracker serves - absolute bangers. 40-40 Deuce.

> *"Bhai crazy clutch mara hai"* - some guy from Kanpur

Two more points followed and it was boys' first match win in the tournament. Gandhinagar's second player was not as good as their first one, doubles match was pocketed easily.

Girls' next match against Hyderabad was a do or die as well. Similar scenes in their group : Kanpur will belt everyone, BHU will get belted by everyone. Hyderabad only had one player who knew her groundstrokes, the second player was void of fundamentals. We knew that we were going to go through, the question was whether in two matches or three?

Idhaa has the potential to beat the heck out of all the girls that were present in the tournament. She is physically superior and her technique is on point. It's only her who can stop her and that's what happened. She gets nervous on courts - very nervous. She'd blow up a single point and her entire game would collapse, as if she forgot how to play.

During the first singles against Hyderabad, everyone was continually shouting from the sidelines, trying to keep her morale up in difficult situations. She tried her best but eventually lost. However, girls got the doubles after exploiting Hyderabad's weak link and then Krishna sealed the victory in the decider singles match.

More or less, both the teams were through group stage.

**matchday 3**

Now, Jodhpur was an easy opponent and Sir wanted to rest Sibi for quarters. It was finally our turn, the third guy and the fourth guy will finally make some good use of their racquet. We had two options :

> 1. Pranav plays the first singles and Hans/Me or Pranav/Sibi for the doubles. 
> 
> 2. Hans plays the first singles and Pranav/Me or Pranav/Sibi for the doubles.

We went with the first one. The Jodhpur guy was an easy haramball merchant and Pranav got the better of him in straight sets. Hans and I were up next. Now, we both are decently skilled, but this was the first time we were stepping on the court after that short practice session on Day 0. We lacked rhythm.

We started off well and took a 4-2 lead in the first set. It went crazy downhill from then on. We were missing volleys, our groundstrokes were going out, into the net and nerves caught us bad. We lost 10 fucking games in a row. Final score : 4-6, 0-6. I feel ashamed of myself as I write this, but yeah, I now have a 100% loss rate at the Inter IIT Sports Meet. Hans has three more tries, he'll do better, I am sure of that.

They gave us one bagel and Sibi returned them two in the decider singles.

Post match, we discussed that option no 2. might have actually won us the matchup in straight matches.

Girls had their final group stage match against Kanpur. The Kanpur girl, who was easily the best singles' player in the tournament, took down Idhaa in the first match effortlessly. The second player was not as good as the first one but she knew how to keep the ball in play. Also, during doubles, Idhaa and Krishna got into a feud which didn't help either. Kanpur beat us, as expected.
